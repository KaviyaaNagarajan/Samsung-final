**Early Warning Signals to Monitor**

1. **Price War Intensity Index (PWII):** Monitor Xiaomi/OnePlus price cuts to determine intensity of price war.
2. **Market Share Movement Index (MSMI):** Track Samsung's market share growth/loss to determine effectiveness of strategies.
3. **Brand Sentiment Score (BSS):** Monitor negative/positive brand perceptions to determine impact on sales.
4. **Component Shortage Index (CSI):** Track component availability to assess supply chain risks.
5. **Competitor Counter-Move Index (CCMI):** Monitor Xiaomi/OnePlus counter-marketing campaigns to determine effectiveness of strategies.

**Mitigation and Contingency Strategies**

1. **Price Adjustment:** Reduce prices of Galaxy S series by 6% to maintain competitiveness.
2. **Feature Enhancements:** Introduce Galaxy A series with improved cameras and processors to target mid-range segment.
3. **Marketing Shift:** Launch targeted marketing campaigns highlighting Samsung's ecosystem advantages (Galaxy Watch, Buds, One UI).
4. **Supply Chain Diversification:** Develop strategic relationships with key suppliers to mitigate supply chain risks.
5. **Counter-Marketing Campaign:** Develop counter-marketing strategy to maintain brand image and engage in public relations efforts to build trust.

**Overall Risk Posture Assessment**

Samsung's overall risk posture is rated as 6.8/10, indicating a moderate level of risk exposure. While Samsung has a strong market position in India, the potential for price war, brand dilution, supply chain shocks, aggressive competitor retaliation, and regulatory or market sentiment risks exist. To mitigate these risks, Samsung should focus on maintaining a hybrid strategy of price adjustments and feature enhancements, diversify its supply chain, and engage in targeted marketing campaigns to highlight its ecosystem advantages.
